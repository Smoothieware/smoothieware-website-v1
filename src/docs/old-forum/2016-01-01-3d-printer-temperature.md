## 3D Printer Temperature

### John flying - 07 Jul 2015 05:17

Just set your temp to 200 to begin with. Although, if you need 210 to get a good part you won't be happy with the result. Even 5 degrees is a substantial enough difference to affect print quality. I suppose you could set the print at 200 and then once printing override to get to 210 but the quality of the first layer is very important. For instance, some materials need a higher temp to adhere the part to the bed. Popping the part off mid print because of poor first layer adhesion is never a good thing.
